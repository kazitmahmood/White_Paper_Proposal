\section{Intellectual Merit}

\paragraph{Challenge.}
Lithium-ion battery thermal runaway is a persistent safety risk in transportation, grid storage, and defense. Interior hot-spot temperatures precede external warning signs by minutes to hours, yet all deployed monitoring technologies require penetrative wiring through the cell housing or measure surface temperature only. Non-contact ultrasonic time-of-flight (TOF) has shown promise as a non-invasive thermometry channel~\cite{Cheng2024}, but TOF is a single-channel, amplitude-agnostic observable that drifts with coupling-layer condition, electrolyte gas evolution, and drive-level variation, limiting practical accuracy to $\pm$1--2$^\circ$C even under controlled conditions.

\paragraph{PI Background.}
The PI's research expertise spans the intersection of nonlinear vibration, acoustics, and periodic elastic structure theory, providing the experimental and analytical foundations this project requires. Prior work demonstrates phase-sensitive lock-in detection of nonlinear harmonic states in piezo-driven acoustic waveguides~\cite{Mahmood2022} and a Bloch-sphere dispersion formalism for one-dimensional elastic lattices covering band-edge analysis and symmetry-governed mode hybridization~\cite{Mahmood2026a}. These contributions establish working expertise in harmonic balance, Floquet-Bloch dispersion, multiple-scales perturbation, and the experimental measurement of harmonic generation in acoustic systems---precisely the toolkit the proposed work requires.

\paragraph{Proposed Approach.}
We propose to exploit the amplitude-dependent band-edge response of a deliberately nonlinear periodic plate waveguide as a thermometry modality that is intrinsically more sensitive and more drift-resistant than linear TOF. A one-dimensional periodic plate waveguide is the structural analog of the coupled elastic lattice studied in~\cite{Mahmood2026a}: alternating unit cells produce pass bands and stop bands whose edge frequencies depend on unit-cell stiffness and mass. Near the band edge the group velocity approaches zero (slow-wave condition), and wave energy accumulates in the unit cells. This dwelling-time amplification greatly enhances second-harmonic generation: at fixed drive amplitude $A_f$ the second-harmonic amplitude satisfies $A_{2f}/A_f \propto \delta_\mathrm{NL}/\Delta^2$, where $\delta_\mathrm{NL}$ is the Duffing stiffness coefficient and $\Delta$ is the frequency detuning from the band edge~\cite{Lazarov2007}. Temperature shifts Young's modulus ($\approx-40$~ppm/$^\circ$C for aluminum), shifting the band-edge frequency and hence $\Delta$; the $1/\Delta^2$ amplification means small temperature changes produce disproportionately large changes in $A_{2f}/A_f$, far exceeding linear-TOF sensitivity. This is qualitatively different from conventional ultrasonic thermometry: the periodic structure acts as a nonlinear amplifier that converts a small stiffness change into a large harmonic ratio change. Differential readout between the TOF reference channel and the nonlinear ratio $A_{2f}/A_f$ additionally suppresses common-mode drive-level and coupling-layer drift, providing a second layer of robustness. Unit-cell nonlinearity is introduced by CNC-machining asymmetric stubs into the plate, producing Duffing-type amplitude-dependent stiffness well within standard machining tolerances.

\paragraph{Why This is New and NewFoS Alignment.}
Linear ultrasonic battery thermometry exists~\cite{Cheng2024}, but using band-edge amplification of second-harmonic generation as a temperature discriminator has not been explored. The critical novelty is treating the periodic elastic structure not as a passive wave conduit but as an active sensing element whose nonlinear frequency response encodes temperature through the $1/\Delta^2$ amplification. The slow-wave nonlinear enhancement mechanism~\cite{Lazarov2007} is established theoretically but has never been deployed as a sensing modality. Wayne State University is a full NewFoS partner; this project extends the Center's acoustic sensing thrust by introducing nonlinear dynamics as a new design lever and broadens its impact through an energy-storage safety application.


\section{Research Plan}

\paragraph{Objectives.}
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
  \item Derive the temperature dependence of $A_{2f}/A_f$ in a nonlinear diatomic 1D chain via harmonic balance and multiple-scales analysis; identify the detuning regime maximizing thermal sensitivity.
  \item Validate via finite element simulation of the plate-waveguide geometry; map the nonlinear ratio as a function of stub geometry, drive amplitude, frequency, and temperature (20--100$^\circ$C).
  \item Demonstrate experimentally that a fabricated nonlinear periodic plate achieves higher temperature sensitivity and lower drift error than a matched linear-TOF control.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Approach.}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Task~1 --- Analytical model (months 1--4):} Apply harmonic balance to a 1D diatomic chain with Duffing inter-cell springs to derive $\partial(A_{2f}/A_f)/\partial T$ as a function of detuning $\Delta = f_0 - f_\mathrm{edge}(T)$ and identify the optimal drive-frequency operating point. Harmonic balance, multiple-scales, and Floquet-Bloch dispersion are active in the PI's current research~\cite{Mahmood2026a}.

  \item \textbf{Task~2 --- FE simulation (months 3--8):} Implement a 30--40\,cm aluminum plate waveguide (8--12 unit cells, asymmetric stubs) in a finite element environment. Extract amplitude-dependent dispersion, map $A_{2f}/A_f$ vs.\ temperature via temperature-dependent material properties, and optimize stub asymmetry for maximum sensitivity; benchmark against Task~1.

  \item \textbf{Task~3 --- Fabrication and experiment (months 6--12):} CNC-machine the plate; bond 500\,kHz piezoelectric transducers; drive with a 20-cycle tone-burst; acquire at 24-bit resolution; compute $A_{2f}/A_f$ and TOF in software over 20--100$^\circ$C ($5^\circ$C steps). Quantify sensitivity (dB/$^\circ$C) and drift rejection under $\pm$20\% imposed drive-level perturbations. A symmetric-stub control sample, fabricated alongside the main specimen, confirms that enhanced sensitivity originates from the band-edge nonlinear mechanism rather than transducer or bond nonlinearity.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Milestones and deliverables.}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{M1 (month 4):} Closed-form temperature-sensitivity model derived; draft manuscript submitted.
  \item \textbf{M2 (month 8):} Finite element sensitivity parameter map complete; results presented at a conference.
  \item \textbf{M3 (month 12):} Plate specimen fabricated and tested; sensitivity and drift-rejection data collected; journal manuscript submitted.
  \item \textbf{Deliverables:} Open-source finite element model, CNC fabrication drawings, raw calibration datasets, and two peer-reviewed manuscripts.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Risk and timeline.}
Primary risk---stub nonlinearity too small to produce a measurable second-harmonic ratio---is mitigated by (a)~a symmetric-stub control for common-mode cancellation, (b)~stub geometry optimization in Task~2 before machining, and (c)~input harmonic filters at the transmitter to suppress source spurious harmonics. \textbf{Timeline}: Sept--Dec~2026 analytical modeling; Jan--Apr~2027 finite element simulation and plate fabrication; May--Aug~2027 experiments, data analysis, and manuscript writing.


\section{Broader Impact}

\paragraph{Technological.}
This project establishes a new acoustic sensing principle in which temperature sensitivity is structurally amplified by slow-wave band-edge dynamics in a nonlinear periodic structure, rather than derived from a direct wave-speed measurement. The modality generalizes from temperature to mechanical strain, state-of-charge, and electrolyte degradation by redesigning the unit-cell geometry to shift the band edge in response to the target quantity. Successful demonstration opens follow-on funding pathways through NSF CMMI, DoE Vehicle Technologies Office, and DoD ONR, and a Year-2 effort will validate the sensor against commercial Li-ion cells under charge-discharge cycling.

\paragraph{Workforce and education.}
This project will develop the PI's expertise at the intersection of nonlinear vibration theory, periodic elastic structure design, acoustic instrumentation, and applied sensing---a rare and commercially valuable combination spanning the aerospace, automotive, and energy-storage industries. Undergraduate research opportunities will be targeted at NewFoS partner Spelman College. All finite element models, analysis scripts, and fabrication drawings will be released as open-source resources through the NewFoS community data portal.
